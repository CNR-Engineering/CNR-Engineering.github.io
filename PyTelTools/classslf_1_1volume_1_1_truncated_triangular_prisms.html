Detailed Description

The representation of Mesh2D in Serafin file when one computes the volume inside polygons of some variables.

A truncated triangular prism is a triangular prism with non-parallel bases. Its geometrical properties are entirely determined by the base triangles.

A prism is constructed, on the top of the base triangle, by giving the height of the nodes in the upper triangle, called the values of the triangle.

A prism can be intersected with a 2D polygon. In this case, only the base triangle is considered.

The volume of the prism is always equal to the surface area of the base triangle times the interpolated value of the centroid of the upper triangle. When intersected with a 2D polygon, the volume of the intersection is equal to the surface area of the intersection (polygon or multipolygon) times the interpolated value of the centroid of the intersection.
